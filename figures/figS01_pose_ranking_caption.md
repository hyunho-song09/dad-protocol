# Supplementary Figure S1 Caption — Nature Protocols Format

**Supplementary Figure S1 | Within-case CNN pose ranking and its diagnostic limitation.**
Bar plot showing the index position of the lowest-RMSD pose within the 9-mode GNINA search output for each of 16 RCSB seed redocking cases. The x-axis lists all 16 case identifiers (protein/PDB/ligand); the y-axis shows the best-of-9 pose index (1 = CNN ranks the best pose first; > 1 = a lower-RMSD pose exists but is not the CNN top-ranked pose). Bars are coloured by top-pose success status (green = PASS, RMSD ≤ 2.0 Å; vermillion = FAIL, RMSD > 2.0 Å); hatching (///) indicates cases where best-of-9 index > 1. The horizontal reference line at index = 1 marks the ideal case where the CNN-ranked top pose is also the geometrically best pose. **Seven of 16 cases (44%)** have best-pose index > 1: TAR_ASP_2LIG (index 2), TAR_ASP_1VLT (index 4), LBP_PHE_1USI (index 2), LBP_LEU_1USK (index 2), MBP_GLC_1ANF (index 8), MBP_GLC_3MBP (index 7), and RBSB_RIB_3KSM (index 4). This is a strictly larger set than the 4/16 top-pose failures: only 4 of these 7 within-case ranking imperfections also fail the 2.0 Å top-pose criterion (TAR_ASP_1VLT, MBP_GLC_1ANF, MBP_GLC_3MBP, RBSB_RIB_3KSM), which means CNN within-case ranking is imperfect even on cases that ultimately pass at the protocol's primary metric. This figure motivates future integration of consensus clustering, PoseBusters filtering, or PLIP-based interaction fingerprints as reference-free pose-selection alternatives (see `dad/core/docking.py::select_consensus_pose`). Best-of-9 RMSD is used throughout this paper exclusively as a *diagnostic* search-recovery metric, not as a prospective operational success rate. Source data: `Publication/supplementary/SI_data/SI_data_rcsb_seed_redocking.tsv` (column `best_pose_index`).
