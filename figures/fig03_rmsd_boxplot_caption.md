**Figure 3 | GNINA redocking RMSD across 16 open RCSB co-crystal cases, stratified by protein family.**
Box-and-whisker plots show the distribution of top-pose RMSD (blue; primary operational metric) and diagnostic best-of-9 RMSD (sky blue; search-recovery metric) for four protein families: CRP/FNR regulators (n = 4), MCP ligand-binding domain (n = 2), amino-acid-binding proteins (n = 3), and periplasmic sugar-binding proteins (n = 7). The dashed red line marks the 2.0 Å success threshold. Individual data points are overlaid as jittered dots. Red triangles annotated with arrows indicate top-pose failures (RMSD > 2.0 Å). Top-pose success: 12/16 cases (75%); best-of-9 search recovery: 15/16 (94%). The CRP/FNR family achieves near-perfect top-pose accuracy (all < 0.4 Å, median 0.29 Å), consistent with its rigid cyclic-nucleotide-binding cleft. The periplasmic sugar-binding protein family shows the highest variance with three top-pose failures (MBP_GLC_1ANF RMSD = 5.12 Å, MBP_GLC_3MBP RMSD = 5.00 Å, RBSB_RIB_3KSM RMSD = 12.78 Å), attributable to a combination of large hinge-motion conformational change (MBP venus-flytrap; true search failure for 1ANF), within-case CNN ranking failure (3MBP, 3KSM; lower-RMSD pose exists in the 9-mode pool), and symmetric OH H-bond ambiguity that flattens the energy landscape. The MCP family contributes one within-case ranking failure (TAR_ASP_1VLT, top RMSD = 3.01 Å, best-of-9 RMSD = 1.63 Å). Redocking parameters: GNINA 1.3.2, exhaustiveness = 32, num_modes = 9, seed = 0; RMSD evaluated as receptor-frame heavy-atom in-place RMSD with RDKit symmetry correction. Source data: `Publication/supplementary/SI_data/SI_data_rcsb_seed_redocking.tsv`; aggregate consistency check: `Publication/tables/table5_family_coverage.csv`.
